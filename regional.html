Published: 24 January 2023

Kudos to the 4 DepEd SDOs for their success in the PRIME-HRM!

The four (4) agencies which have undergone and completed  the PRIME-HRM journey  to Maturity Level 2 in 2022, are now officially recognized and  included in  the elite roster of CSC PRIME-HRM Bronze Awardees.

For the year 2022, CSC-RO1 assessed, assisted and recommended four (4) government agencies for the conferment of the PRIME-HRM Bronze Award for having substantially complied with the Maturity Level 2 System, Competency and Practice indicators and standards in the four (4) Human Resource Management (HRM) areas, namely: Recruitment Selection and Placement (RSP), Learning and Development (L&D), Performance Management (PM), and Rewards and Recognition (R&R) under the Enhanced Program to Institutionalize Meritocracy and Excellence in Human Resource Management (PRIME-HRM). Moreover, these agencies have successfully undergone the processes, including the on-site assessment, to qualify for this prestigious recognition.

The four (4) government agencies are as follows:

       Agency                                            Legal Basis                         Date

  1. DepEd-SDO Batac City             CSC Res. No. 2200772      29 Dec 2022
  2. DepEd-SDO La Union               CSC Res. No. 2200773      29 Dec 2022
  3. DepEd-SDO Candon City          CSC Res. No. 2200774     29 Dec 2022
  4. DepEd-SDO Dagupan City        CSC-Res No. 2200787      29 Dec. 2022 

The PRIME-HRM Bronze Award was conferred to the said agencies for having managed and implemented their human resource management systems according to documented processes, thereby achieving a process-defined HRM in the four core HRM systems.  Their respective CSC Resolutions were signed by CSC Chairperson Atty. Karlo A.B. Nograles, Commissioner Atty. Aileen Lourdes A. Lizada and Commissioner Atty. Ryan Alvin R. Acosta.

The PRIME-HRM journey of these four (4) Schools Division Offices were spearheaded by their respective Schools Division Superintendent (SDS) and Assistant Schools Division Superintendent (ASDS), namely:   SDS Joel B. Lopez and ASDS Annie D. Pagdilao for SDO Batac City, SDS Atty. Donato D. Balderas, Jr.  and former ASDS Vivian Luz S. Pagatpatan (now OIC SDS of SDO Alaminos City) for SDO La Union, SDS Anselmo R. Aludino and ASDS Mariecon G. Ramirez for SDO Candon City, and SDS Aguedo C. Fernandez and ASDS Marciano U. Soriano, Jr. for SDO Dagupan City.

The achievement of these four (4) agencies is truly a remarkable milestone in PRIME-HRM, a flagship program of the Civil Service Commission, particularly in Region 1.  Indeed their success serves as an inspiration for other government agencies aspiring to experience transformation to a higher and more progressive level of human resource management and governance redounding to public service excellence through the PRIME-HRM.

CSC ROI relaunches COMEX

Details
Published: 24 November 2022

The Civil Service Commission Regional Office I conducted the Computerized Examination (COMEX) on November 24, 2022 at the CSC RO1 Building, City of San Fernando, La Union. This is the first computerized exam since its launching in 2017 due to some technical issues and the construction of the new CSC RO1 building. A total of 17 examinees from various agencies in the region took the exam.  

The CSC - COMEX is a joint project between the Department of Science and Technology - Advanced Science and Technology Institute (DOST â€“ ASTI) and the Civil Service Commission (CSC). The exam software unifies all existing standalone examination processes into a single examination system relative to acceptance of applications, conduct of examination, scoring, and generation of reports. This system is implemented throughout all CSC regional offices to accommodate more applicants via computerized mode of testing.

For more information on the said examination, please visit comex.csc.gov.ph.
